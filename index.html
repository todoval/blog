About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher at Charles University in Prague, Czech Republic, where I am part of the Computer Graphics Group. I completed my Ph.D. under the supervision of Alexander Wilkie, with my dissertation focused on the topic of spectral uplifting and advancements in the field. As part of my doctoral studies, I also spent six months at Weta Digital in Wellington, New Zealand, where I gained valuable experience in integrating research into production rendering pipelines.

In addition, my work focuses on light transport, spectral rendering, material appearance, color science, and also extends to volume rendering, image-based lighting (IBL), and HDR imaging.

At present, I continue to pursue research on material appearance and investigate how results of spectral light transport can be used to advance other aspects of rendering. I also remain open to new directions and actively seek collaborations.
